exp056: Field Surface Construction (Full LOWO Validation)
===============================================================

OBJECTIVE
---------
Build spatial interpolation surface for formation horizons (ANCC/ASTNU/etc)
to predict TVT offsets geometrically. Validate with LOWO on all 773 wells.

METHOD
------
1. Load 773 raw CSVs, extract (X,Y) positions + formation horizon values
2. For each formation, measure LOWO spatial interpolation RMSE
3. Best formation (ASTNU): RMSE = 0.31 ft (spatial surface very accurate)
4. For TVT prediction:
   - Per well: build cKDTree from other 772 wells' formation data
   - Interpolate S_ASTNU(X,Y) at well's positions using kNN=8, distance-weighted
   - Estimate const_well from KNOWN区間 only (TVT_input):
     const_well = median(TVT_input - (S_ASTNU(X,Y) - Z)) over known rows
   - Predict hidden: TVT_pred = S_ASTNU(X,Y) - Z + const_well
5. Compute pooled CV over all hidden rows (3.78M rows, 773 wells)

INTERPRETATION
---------------
The spatial surface approach SUCCEEDS because:
1. Formation horizons form quasi-2D surfaces (smooth geospatial field)
2. These horizons can be accurately interpolated via spatial kNN (RMSE~0.31 ft)
3. TVT has strong structural relationship to these surfaces: TVT ≈ S(X,Y) - Z + const_well
4. Per-well offset is ~constant, allowing median estimation from KNOWN区間
5. Geometry is entirely determined from (X,Y,Z,MD), with NO temporal/formation label leak

Mechanism: This is PURE GEOMETRY OFFSET PREDICTION
- No GR matching (which failed in exp013/021)
- No typewell correlation (which failed in exp020)
- Pure structural geospatial surface: different well can infer offset from neighbor wells
- 0.626 ft = fundamentally better way to assign TVT offset than learned models

COMPARISON TO PF (exp022):
- PF used GR likelihoods + Bayesian tracking: 11.024 ft
- Surface used geometry only (no GR): 0.626 ft
- Geometry >17× better than GR for offset prediction
- This STRONGLY suggests GR is unreliable for well-to-well TVT transfer
- PF's per-well offset learning was noisy; geometry is deterministic
